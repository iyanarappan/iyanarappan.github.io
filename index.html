Tamilnadu - India

Iyanar is a village god in Tamil Nadu. Iyanar is also worshipped by Lankan Tamils as Ayyanayake and Ayappa in Kerala. He is primarily worshipped as a guardian deity who protects the rural villages. Most officiating priests are derived from local potter community but other caste members also officiate in his temples. The temples of Iyanar are usually flanked by gigantic and colourful structures of him and his companions riding horses or elephants.

The tamil word Iyanar is derived from the root word ‘Aiya’ which is a title often used by Tamils, Malayalis and Telugus to designate respectable people. Other names of Iyanar inclue Gajavahana and Satavahana.

Literary references to Ayanar-Cattan is formed in the Silapathikaram, a Tamil Jain Work dated to the 4th to 5th century. From the Chola period (9th century) onwards the popularity of Iyanar became even more pronounced.

As Ayanar is usually represented either as a warior on foot; or riding a white horse or element. He is also represented as seated between his two wives Porkalai & Purani carrying a sceptre or whip and wearing a meditation band, a cloth girdle circling the back and supporting the knees of a seated person. The attendant Priest for the Iyanar is generally the potter who fashions idols and clay horses and is a non-vegeterian.

The festivals of Iyanar are celebrated in sacred Groves during spring season by all the related clan. Iyanar shrines are usually located at the peripheries or boundaries of rural villages and the deity is seen riding horse with a sword. Weapons such as trident or a lance are also associated with the shrine.

The worship pattern is agamic. The Agamas are non Vedic in origin and is associated with sacrificial offerings of vegetarian food. However animals such as chicken or goats are offered.

Kaval deivangal such as karuppasamy Sudalai Maadan Samy, Madurai Veeran and other Amman deities located within Iyanar temple for favours. In temple Priest will offer holly Veeputhi to the worshippers. During festivals, fold tales like koothu and fold arts like villupattu are enacted to bring out the message of Ayannar fold story to one and all.
